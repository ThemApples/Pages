Update Blog

November 2020

Yesterday, I decided to change my operating system because something was causing my laptop to heat up quickly and to slow down my processors. I confirmed this by using the htop command on my terminal my processors were pushing hard even though nothing was active. I'm assuming it was the hardware requirements. As a result, I searched online for an operating system that can work on old hardware to test whether my theory was right.

I search for an Operating systems distribution for an hour. There were a lot of them that require faster hardware. That was disappointing wasting an hour and not finding something that will run well on a laptop. I remember a few YouTubers saying an XFCE based operating system would work well with older hardware.

I found one that was interesting to me, called Xubuntu. I tested the live session to see whether it was the same as the Youtuber said. I also had to check whether screen tearing was present for functionality purposes.

I installed the operating system onto my laptop. The installation didn't take very long to finish. Everything was functioning properly until I attempted to modernize the appearance of the operating system. I log out of my laptop to see if the log out functionality was working. It didn't work on the operating system it would loop presenting a blank screen and my login page. whenever the login pape showed up I tried to log in but the screen would change to the blank immediately.

I was stuck on this problem for around 2 hours. I searched online to find a solution but I could not find one. I ended up getting an older build and installing that. It worked. Everything was functional.
